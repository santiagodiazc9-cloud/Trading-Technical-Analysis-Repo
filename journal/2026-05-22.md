## Lessons
- **A fill is not done until it's in `trade_log.json`.** The GOOGL entry (5/20) lived only in `open_positions.md`; `trades[]` was empty and `weekly_trade_count` read 0 — so the weekly-trade-count guardrail and any calibration math were silently running on wrong data. The market-open execution routine that placed the order never logged it. Reconciled at this EOD. The buy and the trade-log write must be atomic in the execution routine — candidate hardening item for Friday's weekly review.
- **Holding a swing through a scheduler blackout is the real cost of the infra gap.** Earlier infra gaps only cost us missed scans (zero positions, nothing at risk). Now there's capital deployed. A day with no routine (5/21) is now a day with an unmonitored position and an unverified stop. The scheduler fix moved from "annoying" to "risk-relevant."
- **Catalyst + oversold is a thesis, not a guarantee.** GOOGL had the textbook confluence (I/O catalyst + Stoch 0.06) and still drifted down through a rallying market. The 6/10 confidence score correctly reflected the uncertainty — the catalyst gave a *reason* for a bounce, not a *promise* of one.
