## Lessons
- Every routine that places an order MUST, in the same run, (1) update `open_positions.md` Current Positions, (2) append the trade to `trade_log.json`, and (3) increment `weekly_trade_count`. The 5/20 routine did (1) only. A single-step omission silently corrupts the weekly-trade-limit accounting — a hard risk rule. Worth a post-entry checklist assertion.
- The market-open routine is the right place to reconcile memory against Alpaca: it reads the broker state first thing anyway, so any divergence (positions, counts) surfaces cheaply.
